Security Details

In the last two years, nearly 90% of healthcare organizations faced a breach; on average, 17,000 patient records are breached every day.

There’s no getting around the fact that ransomware attacks on healthcare providers are going to get worse before they get better, meaning the need for a robust IT department providing comprehensive, proactive solutions is all the more essential. The cost of just one hack averages out to $2.2 million per organization, not to mention the costs associated with loss of patient loyalty, damage to reputation, lawsuits, and legal fees. As a healthcare provider, the data you work with represents a veritable treasure trove that cybercriminals are just itching to get their hands on; just one medical record fetches an average of $363 on the black market, which is higher than the price of data from any other industry. Despite these threats:

  • • 74% of healthcare practices are not encrypting data on mobile devices
  • • 60% of practices do not implement two-factor authentication
  • • 47% of practices using the cloud are not confident in their ability to keep data secure.
  • • 50% of organizations’ 3 rd party applications have been rated “always vulnerable” by WhiteHat Security.

This lack of attention to security might go a long way towards explaining why 45% of practices reported having more than 5 breaches within the last 2 years. Data breaches aren’t good for you, they’re not good for your practice, and they’re certainly not good for your patients. If you’re concerned about the security of your practice, schedule a free security assessment with our team today.
